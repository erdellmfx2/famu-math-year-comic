# Week 01 Episode 01 — Before the First Bell

## Source
- Timeline: `story/timeline-weeks/1.md`
- Prose: `story/timeline-weeks-prose/prose_1.md`

## Episode Purpose
Introduce Malik, establish the pre-semester FAMU atmosphere, and begin the Malik/Nia dynamic with a memorable first exchange.

## Episode Format
- **Recommended format:** 4-panel daily comic
- **Primary viewpoint:** Malik
- **Setting:** outdoor campus common area near the start of the semester
- **Emotional arc:** focused solitude -> interruption -> curiosity -> connection

## Character Notes for This Episode
- Malik is calm, organized, slightly intense, but not cold.
- Nia arrives with warmth, wit, and immediate social ease.
- Their first interaction should feel natural, smart, and lightly funny.

## Panel Script

### Panel 1 — Establishing the pressure before classes
**Visual:** Warm Florida afternoon on FAMU campus. Red brick buildings, light student foot traffic, green trees, golden sunlight. Malik sits alone at a small outdoor table with an open laptop showing a spreadsheet. A notebook and calculator are nearby.

**Caption:** "Before classes even started, Malik was already doing the math."

### Panel 2 — Malik’s mindset
**Visual:** Medium shot over Malik’s shoulder. Spreadsheet visible with categories like meal plan, textbooks, supplies, and an emphasized line for unexpected costs. Malik’s brow is furrowed in concentration.

**Caption:** "If he could learn the pattern early, maybe the year wouldn’t catch him off guard."

### Panel 3 — Nia enters with personality
**Visual:** Nia appears beside the table, tote bag on shoulder, notebook under one arm, amused smile on her face. Malik looks up, slightly surprised.

**Dialogue (Nia):** "That spreadsheet looks like it pays taxes for fun."

### Panel 4 — First spark of connection
**Visual:** Two-shot of Malik and Nia with the empty chair between them or Nia beginning to sit. Malik has the smallest half-smile. Campus continues softly in the background.

**Dialogue (Malik):** "I’m not sure whether that’s an insult."
**Dialogue (Nia):** "It’s concern."

## End Tag Options
- "Next: the planner meets the spreadsheet."
- "First impressions matter."
- "Two math minds, two very different systems."

## Visual Priorities
- Make the campus feel warm and alive
- Make Malik’s structure immediately visible through props and posture
- Make Nia’s entrance feel bright and disarming
- Keep panel 4 emotionally sticky enough to hook the next post
