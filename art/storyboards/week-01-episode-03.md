# Week 01 Episode 03 — Why math at FAMU?

## Source
- Timeline: `story/timeline-weeks/1.md`
- Prose: `story/timeline-weeks-prose/prose_1.md`

## Episode Purpose
Move Malik and Nia from introductions into a real values conversation by revealing why each of them chose math at FAMU.

## Episode Format
- **Recommended format:** 4-panel daily comic
- **Primary viewpoint:** shared Malik/Nia two-hander
- **Setting:** same outdoor campus table as Episodes 01-02
- **Emotional arc:** light banter -> honest curiosity -> shared purpose -> stronger connection

## Panel Script

### Panel 1 — Nia asks the real question
**Visual:** Seated two-shot at the same campus table. Nia leans in with genuine curiosity while Malik sits with one hand near the laptop. Keep the spreadsheet, notebook, and calculator visible for continuity.

**Dialogue (Nia):** "So why actuarial science?"

### Panel 2 — Malik answers with intention
**Visual:** Medium shot on Malik, calm and thoughtful rather than defensive. Warm campus brick and greenery stay soft in the background.

**Dialogue (Malik):** "I like patterns. And I like knowing math can protect people from expensive surprises."

### Panel 3 — Nia shares her side
**Visual:** Medium shot on Nia, bright but sincere, notebook still nearby. She should look excited by ideas, not just joking.

**Dialogue (Nia):** "I like math when it helps people see what’s possible."

### Panel 4 — FAMU makes the connection land
**Visual:** Warm conversational two-shot. Both are a little more relaxed now, with an easy sense that they have found common ground. Leave negative space for balloons.

**Dialogue (Malik):** "So you’re an optimist with color coding."
**Dialogue (Nia):** "And you’re a realist with spreadsheets. At least FAMU gave us both a table."
