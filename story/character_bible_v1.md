## Character 1: Malik Baptiste

### Core Identity
- **Role:** Male first-year student
- **Major:** Actuarial Science
- **Track:** Quantitative risk, finance, predictive modeling
- **Hometown vibe:** Caribbean-rooted, practical, family-first, quietly competitive

### Bio
Malik arrives at FAMU with a clear mission: become an actuary and build long-term financial stability for his family. He’s disciplined, detail-oriented, and naturally good with patterns, but college pushes him to grow from “good at math” into “good at math under pressure, deadlines, and uncertainty.” He’s serious about internships, professional exams, and building a resume early.

### Personality & Voice
- Calm, focused, strategic
- Thinks in plans and probabilities
- Dry humor, not loud but sharp
- Speaks clearly and briefly; avoids drama

### Academic Profile
- Strong in algebra/statistics reasoning
- Working to improve proof-style thinking and conceptual explanations
- **Key courses in story year:** MAC 2311 → MAC 2312 → MAC 2313 path, plus intro stats/finance-aligned classes

### Typical Weekly Activities (Comic-friendly)
- Early library sessions before class
- Group problem-solving before calculus quizzes
- Tutoring-center check-ins before major exams
- Internship/career prep events and faculty office-hour visits

### Arc (Year 1)
1. **Fall start:** Confident but rigid
2. **Mid-fall stress:** First major grade shock; learns to ask for help
3. **Spring growth:** Becomes a dependable study-group anchor
4. **Year-end shift:** Moves from “I must be perfect” to “I can improve consistently”

### Visual Direction
- Colors: deep green, charcoal, white
- Props: graph notebook, financial calculator, laptop with spreadsheet tabs
- Expression range: focused brow, half-smile when solving hard problems

## Character 2: Nia Reynolds

### Core Identity
- **Role:** Female first-year student
- **Major:** Mathematical Sciences
- **Track:** Broad math foundation (pure + applied), future research/teaching/data pathways
- **Hometown vibe:** North Florida, high-achiever, curious and creative

### Bio
Nia chose Mathematical Sciences because she loves math itself—not just one application. She’s fascinated by how calculus, modeling, and abstraction connect to real life. At FAMU, she quickly becomes known for asking strong questions in class and helping others understand difficult ideas. Her challenge is balancing leadership, academics, and rest.

### Personality & Voice
- Warm, insightful, expressive
- Confident in class discussions
- Encouraging with peers; naturally mentor-like
- Uses analogies to explain hard concepts

### Academic Profile
- Strong conceptual learner and communicator
- Occasionally overcommits due to ambition
- **Key courses in story year:** MAC 2311 → MAC 2312 → MAC 2313 progression, with interest in advanced theory and applications

### Typical Weekly Activities (Comic-friendly)
- Front-row lecture notes and post-class question sessions
- Whiteboard study circles in the library
- Student activities/organization meetings
- Preparing mini review sheets before test weeks

### Arc (Year 1)
1. **Fall start:** Energized, joins everything
2. **Mid-fall overload:** Burnout warning; learns boundaries
3. **Spring clarity:** Prioritizes high-impact commitments
4. **Year-end shift:** Stronger leader with sustainable habits

### Visual Direction
- Colors: orange, green accents, denim/neutral base
- Props: color-coded notes, sticky tabs, tablet for handwritten math
- Expression range: bright curiosity, determined focus, celebratory joy

## Duo Dynamic (Core Engine for Daily Comic)

### Relationship
- Friends and academic accountability partners
- Respect each other’s strengths: Malik = strategy/structure, Nia = concepts/communication
- Light tension: pace and work style differences

### Recurring Scene Types
- “Before-class 5-minute debate” (quick math concept exchange)
- “Post-quiz debrief” (what worked / what failed)
- “Calendar checkpoint” (deadlines, registration, exam week)
- “Campus walk reflections” (growth, purpose, faith/family motivation)

### Signature Contrast
- **Malik asks:** “What’s the most efficient path?”
- **Nia asks:** “What does this mean deeply?”
- Best episodes happen when both questions are answered together.

- Late-semester fatigue and reset habits
