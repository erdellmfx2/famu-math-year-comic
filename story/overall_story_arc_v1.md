# Overall Story Arc v1 — FAMU Math Year Comic

## Series Premise
Two first-year FAMU math students — **Malik Baptiste** (Actuarial Science) and **Nia Reynolds** (Mathematical Sciences) — navigate a full academic year of classes, exams, friendships, setbacks, growth, and purpose. The tone is inspiring, relatable, and G-rated, designed for daily social comic episodes.

---

## Arc Structure (Academic Year)

## ACT I — ARRIVAL & ADJUSTMENT (August–September)
### Theme
New beginnings, identity, and expectations.

### Story Goals
- Introduce Malik, Nia, core professors, and campus rhythm.
- Establish different mindsets:
  - Malik = structure, efficiency, long-term planning
  - Nia = curiosity, community, conceptual depth
- Show first-year friction: pacing, time management, self-doubt.

### Key Milestones
- Move-in / orientation week energy
- First week of classes (MAC 2311 focus)
- First quiz and first "college-level" wake-up moment
- Study-group formation

### Emotional Beat
Optimism meets reality.

---

## ACT II — PRESSURE & PIVOT (October–November)
### Theme
Challenge reveals character.

### Story Goals
- Raise stakes with heavier assignments and midterms.
- Force both students to adapt:
  - Malik learns perfectionism can be a trap.
  - Nia learns overcommitment can drain performance.
- Strengthen mentor roles through office hours and professor guidance.

### Key Milestones
- Midterm season stress
- First major setback (grade disappointment or missed opportunity)
- Tutoring/office-hour turning point
- Friendship tension + recovery (different work styles)
- Career/major-identity episode (why math? why this path?)

### Emotional Beat
Frustration → humility → renewed strategy.

---

## ACT III — FINISH STRONG (Late November–December)
### Theme
Discipline, resilience, and earned confidence.

### Story Goals
- Deliver first-semester payoff:
  - Better study systems
  - Better communication
  - Better emotional regulation
- Show finals week realism with supportive tone.

### Key Milestones
- Finals prep montage episodes
- Professor check-ins and practical advice
- End-of-semester reflections
- Winter break reset and goal-setting for spring

### Emotional Beat
Exhaustion, then pride.

---

## ACT IV — RENEWAL & EXPANSION (January–March)
### Theme
Second-semester confidence and deeper ambition.

### Story Goals
- Move into higher rigor (MAC 2312/2313 progression feel).
- Show growth in leadership and collaboration.
- Introduce future-facing goals: internships, research, long-term pathways.

### Key Milestones
- Strong spring restart
- New challenge in advanced concepts
- Public confidence moment (presentation, peer teaching, org role)
- Career prep and mentoring episodes

### Emotional Beat
“I’m not just surviving college — I belong here.”

---

## ACT V — LEGACY OF YEAR ONE (April–May)
### Theme
Reflection, momentum, and becoming.

### Story Goals
- Close the year with maturity and purpose.
- Show the pair as more grounded, capable, and future-ready.
- Set up Year Two naturally.

### Key Milestones
- Final exam period and closure beats
- Community/family pride episodes
- End-of-year celebration and reflection walk
- Forward-looking stinger: sophomore year preview

### Emotional Beat
Gratitude + next-level ambition.

---

## Character Arc Integration

### Malik (Actuarial Science)
- **Start:** Highly disciplined, overly rigid, fear of mistakes
- **Middle:** Learns to seek help, communicate uncertainty, and adapt
- **End:** Still strategic, now more collaborative and emotionally balanced

### Nia (Mathematical Sciences)
- **Start:** Brilliant and enthusiastic, says yes to everything
- **Middle:** Burnout warning leads to boundary-setting and prioritization
- **End:** Stronger leader with sustainable excellence

### Duo Arc
- **Start:** Respectful but misaligned styles
- **Middle:** Conflict under pressure
- **End:** Complementary partnership; each makes the other better

---

## Professor Arc Function (Story Engine)
- **Dr. Brooks (Calc I):** Transition-to-college rigor mentor
- **Dr. Delgado (Calc II):** Precision and method discipline
- **Dr. Okafor (Calc III):** Conceptual expansion and confidence
- **Dr. Bennett (Stats):** Career realism + actuarial mentorship
- **Dr. Price (Diff Eq):** Systems thinking and future orientation

## Daily Comic Episode Rhythm (Recommended)
- **Mon:** Academic challenge (class/quiz/problem)
- **Tue:** Character moment (friendship, mindset, identity)
- **Wed:** Mentor/professor insight
- **Thu:** Campus life/community beat
- **Fri:** Turning point or mini-cliffhanger
- **Sat:** Reflection/humor/light episode
- **Sun:** Reset/planning/preview episode

## Season 1 Logline
At FAMU, two first-year math majors learn that success is more than solving equations — it’s learning who you are under pressure, who you can trust, and what kind of future you’re brave enough to build.
