# Plot Outline v1 — Adapted from *Anne of Green Gables* Patterns

## Goal
Use proven coming-of-age plot patterns from *Anne of Green Gables* and adapt them to our FAMU first-year math comic year (Aug 2025–Aug 2026).

## Core Patterns Pulled from *Anne*
1. **Belonging arc** — outsider → accepted community member
2. **Mishap-to-growth loop** — mistakes become maturity moments
3. **Academic rivalry/friendly push** — competition drives excellence
4. **Deep friendship anchor** — emotional support through setbacks
5. **Mentor-guided formation** — adults shape values and discipline
6. **Responsibility turning point** — character chooses duty over ego
7. **Optimistic close with next-step momentum**

---

## Year Plot Spine (Comic Adaptation)

## Phase 1 — New Place, New Identity (Aug–Sep)
### Anne-pattern adaptation
- “Arrival + social adjustment”
### FAMU story version
- Malik and Nia enter FAMU with strong math ability but very different habits.
- They both feel pressure to prove they belong in rigorous courses.
- Early class mishaps (overconfidence, poor pacing, missed assumptions) create humility.

### Episode fuel
- Orientation confusion, first class shock, first quiz surprise, first study-group connection.

---

## Phase 2 — Scrapes, Pride, and Learning (Oct–Nov)
### Anne-pattern adaptation
- “Memorable mistakes that trigger growth”
### FAMU story version
- Malik’s precision fails under time pressure; Nia’s overcommitment causes burnout risk.
- A misunderstanding in their study partnership creates tension.
- Professors intervene with practical guidance, not rescue.

### Episode fuel
- Midterm stumbles, office-hour breakthroughs, conflict/reconciliation arc.

---

## Phase 3 — Rivalry Becomes Respect (Nov–Dec)
### Anne-pattern adaptation
- “Rival energy transforms into mutual uplift”
### FAMU story version
- Malik and Nia challenge each other academically in healthy ways.
- They shift from comparison to collaboration before finals.
- Their growth is shown through how they help others, not just grades.

### Episode fuel
- Finals prep, peer tutoring, confidence under pressure, semester close.

---

## Phase 4 — Return Stronger (Jan–Mar)
### Anne-pattern adaptation
- “Matured return with clearer purpose”
### FAMU story version
- Spring starts with stronger routines and clearer boundaries.
- Math difficulty rises (Calc II/III rhythm), but resilience is higher.
- Sports/event-heavy weeks test consistency and emotional discipline.

### Episode fuel
- New-semester reset, difficult concepts, high-pressure overlap days.

---

## Phase 5 — Responsibility Choice (Apr–May)
### Anne-pattern adaptation
- “Choose responsibility and community over personal ego”
### FAMU story version
- Both students face opportunities that conflict with obligations.
- They choose actions that protect long-term growth, team, and family trust.
- End-of-year identity shift: from “students surviving” to “students becoming leaders.”

### Episode fuel
- Finals, family/community pride, mentor acknowledgment, year-end reflection.

---

## Phase 6 — Summer Bridge & Forward Motion (May–Aug)
### Anne-pattern adaptation
- “Hopeful continuation”
### FAMU story version
- Summer terms reinforce discipline and long-view goals.
- They prepare for year two with stronger confidence and clearer pathways.
- Season closes with a teaser for sophomore-level ambition.

### Episode fuel
- Summer class cadence, internships/research prep, future planning scenes.

---

## Character-Level Plot Threads

### Malik (Actuarial Science)
- Learns that control is not the same as mastery.
- Biggest shift: asks for help earlier, communicates uncertainty, leads through consistency.

### Nia (Mathematical Sciences)
- Learns that brilliance needs sustainable structure.
- Biggest shift: from overextension to intentional leadership and focused impact.

### Relationship Thread
- Starts as contrast, becomes partnership.
- Their best wins happen when efficiency (Malik) and depth (Nia) combine.

## “Anne-like” Scene Types to Reuse in Comic Form
- Small social misunderstandings with emotional stakes
- Classroom pride moments followed by humbling correction
- Mentor conversations that redirect identity
- Friendship reconciliation scenes
- Quiet reflection walks after intense academic days
- Final scene each arc: hopeful, grounded, forward-looking
